Build 01 Architecture — as it appears in the Command Center
Architecture — 5 Layers · What They Do · Why They Exist
Every morning, a junior analyst at a £2bn fund opens 12 browser tabs. Reuters. FT. FCA. Two earnings PDFs from their inbox. They spend 90 minutes reading before they've done a single minute of actual work.
This build doesn't replace the analyst. It gives them their morning back. Here's how.
01
INGEST
The Ingesters — RSS · PDF · Web
WHAT IT DOES
Three specialised readers — one for news feeds, one for PDFs, one for websites — each knowing exactly how to pull clean text from its source type.
WHY IT EXISTS
"The world's intelligence doesn't arrive in one format. A rate decision comes as a news headline. A fund's risk exposure sits in a 40-page PDF. A regulator's warning lives on a government website. If your system can only read one of those, you're already behind. This layer speaks all three languages — at the same time."
02
PARALLEL
asyncio.gather() — Parallel Execution
WHAT IT DOES
All three ingesters fire simultaneously. RSS, PDF, and web run at exactly the same time — not one after another.
WHY IT EXISTS
"A human can only read one thing at a time. That's not a flaw — that's just biology. But software doesn't have that constraint. This is the moment the machine stops thinking like a person and starts thinking like a machine. Three sources. One command. Thirty seconds. That's the entire morning brief — done before your coffee is cold."
03
NORMALISE
DocumentNormaliser — One Schema to Rule Them All
WHAT IT DOES
Every document — regardless of source — gets shaped into the exact same structure: source type, URL, title, clean content, timestamp. Token count checked. Nothing bloated goes downstream.
WHY IT EXISTS
"You can't build intelligence on chaos. An RSS feed gives you HTML soup. A PDF gives you raw text with page headers in the middle of sentences. A website gives you nav bars and cookie banners mixed in with the actual content. This layer is the translator. It cleans everything, structures everything, and makes sure every piece of information speaks the same language before it touches the AI."
04
AI LAYER
ClaudeSummariser — The Intelligence Engine
THE MAGIC LAYER
WHAT IT DOES
Sends each normalised document to Claude with a specific brief: summarise this in exactly 3 sentences, for an asset manager, be specific, include numbers, dates, company names. Raw text in. Actionable intelligence out.
WHY IT EXISTS
"Here's where the pipeline stops being a data tool and becomes a thinking tool. Raw text isn't intelligence — it's noise waiting to be processed. A 40-page earnings transcript has three sentences in it that actually matter to a portfolio manager. This layer finds those three sentences. Every single time. At scale. That's not automation — that's leverage."
05
STORE
SQLiteDocumentStore — The Audit Trail
WHAT IT DOES
Every document — source, content, summary, timestamp — written to a structured SQLite database with full indexing. JSON report exported after every run. Nothing is lost. Everything is traceable.
WHY IT EXISTS
"In finance, if it wasn't logged, it didn't happen. The FCA doesn't care that your AI was fast. It cares that you can prove what it saw, when it saw it, and what it did with it. This layer is what makes the whole pipeline FCA-ready — not just useful, but defensible. That's the difference between a cool tool and a production system."
